What is periodontitis?

Periodontitis, also known as gum disease, is a disease of the gums and surrounding tissues caused by plaque accumulation on teeth and under the gums. The disease usually progresses slowly and does not cause pain in the early stages, so many patients do not notice it. Research shows that approximately every second adult faces periodontitis in at least one jaw.

Periodontal treatment – why is it important?

Gum diseases (periodontal diseases) are one of the most common reasons why people lose teeth. Periodontal treatment helps to preserve teeth, strengthen gum and bone tissues, and ensure long-term oral health.

When is periodontal treatment needed?

We recommend consulting if you notice one or more of these symptoms:

Gum Bleeding

Gum bleeding or burning sensation

Gum Recession

Gum recession, exposed "pockets" around teeth

Increased Mobility

Weakened tooth support, tooth mobility

Bad Breath

Bad breath or gum sensitivity, suppuration

Increased Sensitivity

Increased sensitivity or tooth movement without trauma

Early Intervention

Early intervention helps to avoid complex surgical interventions and even tooth loss.
