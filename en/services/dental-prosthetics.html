Dental prosthetics – what is it?

Dental prosthetics is a procedure designed to restore lost, worn or damaged teeth. Modern dentistry allows restoring both a single tooth and the entire jaw, ensuring a natural appearance and functionality.

Prostheses can be fixed (crowns, bridges, veneers) or removable (partial or complete). Fixed prostheses are permanent and attached so that they do not need to be removed, while removable ones – the patient can remove them, but they often cause more inconvenience.

The most advanced solution – prosthetics on implants, which provides stability, natural feeling and long-term result. This is the most modern tooth restoration technique, often called the gold standard in modern dentistry.

When is dental prosthetics needed?

Dental prosthetics is recommended when:

Missing teeth

One or more teeth are missing or all teeth are lost

Damaged teeth

Teeth are chipped, broken or heavily worn

After treatment

After root canal treatment, it is necessary to strengthen the tooth

Aesthetics

It is aimed to improve smile aesthetics – color, shape or alignment

Individual solution

We perform tooth restoration and full jaw prosthetics on implants – individually, comfortably and aesthetically.

Types of Prostheses

Removable prostheses

Easily removable dental prostheses that hold on adjacent tooth clasps or rest on gums.

Fixed prostheses

Fixed bridges and crowns, attached on implants or natural teeth

Prosthetics Types

Choose the most suitable prosthetics method according to Your needs and possibilities.

Complete Dentures

Completely removable dentures, when all teeth in the jaw are lost.

  • Full functional restoration
  • Aesthetic look
  • Easy maintenance

Partial Dentures

When several teeth are lost, but healthy adjacent teeth remain.

  • Safe for adjacent teeth
  • Cheaper than implants
  • Fast production time

Fixed Bridges

Non-removable bridges, attached on adjacent teeth or implants.

  • Maximum stability
  • Natural feeling
  • Long-term solution

Prostheses on Implants

Most reliable solution – prostheses attached on implants.

  • 100% stability
  • Bone protection
  • Longevity

Temporary Dentures

Used while permanent prostheses are being made or implants are healing.

  • Fast production
  • Aesthetics while waiting
  • Functionality

Overdentures

Prostheses that are attached on remaining teeth or implants.

  • Better fixation
  • Better feeling
  • Universality

Frequently Asked Questions

We answer popular questions about dental prosthetics.

Usually prosthesis production takes 1-3 weeks. Temporary prostheses can be made in a few days. Complete dentures production takes longer due to additional fittings. Fast prostheses can be made in 1-2 days.

In the first days, slight discomfort or gum irritation may be felt, but this passes in a few days. Modern prostheses are very comfortable, and after the adaptation period you almost won't feel them.

Prostheses must be cleaned daily with a special brush and paste. It is recommended to remove prostheses at night and keep them in a special solution. Once a year, professional cleaning at the dentist must be performed.

We provide up to 5 years warranty for prostheses, depending on material type. Warranty covers mechanical defects, but not injuries or improper care.

Yes, modern prostheses allow eating most food products. However, it is recommended to avoid very hard or sticky products, especially in the first days. Fixed prostheses allow eating without any restrictions.
