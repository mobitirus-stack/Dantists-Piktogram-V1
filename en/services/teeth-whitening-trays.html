Who is Teeth Whitening For?

Teeth whitening is suitable for most adults who want to improve the aesthetics of their smile.

Ideal Candidates:

  • Healthy teeth without cavities or fissures
  • Healthy gums without bleeding
  • No large fillings or crowns on front teeth
  • No allergies to whitening materials
  • Have realistic expectations

When to Avoid:

  • Pregnancy and breastfeeding period
  • Age younger than 16 years
  • Very high tooth sensitivity
  • Existing dental diseases (caries, periodontitis)
  • Many front teeth fillings or crowns

Frequently Asked Questions

We answer the most popular questions about teeth whitening so you feel confident.

Most patients do not feel pain during whitening. Some may feel slight sensitivity, similar to a cold sensation in the teeth, but it passes immediately after the procedure. Modern methods, such as LED, are especially gentle. In case of increased sensitivity, we can use a special protective gel.

Results last an average of 1-2 years. Longer duration depends on lifestyle: smoking, coffee, tea, red wine, certain medications can darken teeth. To maintain the result, we recommend repeating whitening every 6-12 months and avoiding the most staining products.

Professional teeth whitening, performed in clinical conditions, is safe and does not damage tooth structure. The materials we use are approved and safe. Any short-term sensitivity after the procedure is normal and passes within a few days.

We recommend avoiding food and drinks that can stain teeth (coffee, tea, red wine, chocolate, smoking) for at least 48 hours after the procedure. For the first 24 hours drink only water and eat light, non-hot food.

It depends on the location and material of the fillings. If you have fillings only in the back teeth, whitening will definitely be suitable. If fillings are in the front teeth, they do not whiten, so color unevenness may appear. In this case, we can offer alternatives - filling replacement or dental veneers.
