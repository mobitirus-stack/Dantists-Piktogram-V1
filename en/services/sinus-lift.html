Sinus Lift

Safe upper jaw preparation for implantation.

Book Consultation

Sinus lift is a surgical procedure performed in the upper jaw when there is insufficient bone for dental implant placement. During the procedure, the sinus membrane is lifted, and new bone tissue is formed.

‚ùì What is sinus lift?

The maxillary sinuses are air-filled cavities above the molars. When these teeth are lost, the bone thins, and the sinus "drops". During the sinus lift operation:

  • The sinus membrane is lifted
  • The cavity is filled with bone substitute
  • A foundation for the implant is created
  • Implant stability is ensured

Procedure Methods

Closed Sinus Lift

A less invasive procedure performed when there is a small lack of bone height (3-4 mm). Performed through the implant drilling hole.

  • Less trauma
  • Fast healing
  • Immediate implantation

Open Sinus Lift

Used when there is a significant bone deficiency. A "window" is formed in the side of the jaw. This is a reliable way to create a large bone volume.

  • Large bone reconstruction
  • Maximum safety
  • Healing 6-9 months
