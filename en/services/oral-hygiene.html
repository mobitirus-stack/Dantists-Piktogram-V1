Frequently Asked Questions

We answer the most popular questions about oral hygiene so you can feel confident and know what to expect.

It is recommended to perform professional oral hygiene every 6 months. If you have gum problems, smoke, or take certain medications, more frequent care may be needed - every 3-4 months. Regular hygiene helps prevent cavities, gum diseases, and other oral problems.

Most patients do not feel professional cleaning as a painful procedure. In case of increased sensitivity, local anesthesia can be used. There may be some sensitivity after the procedure, but it passes within 1-2 days. Modern methods, such as Air-Flow, are especially gentle.

Air-Flow is a gentle teeth cleaning method using a special mixture of powder, water, and air. It effectively removes pigmentation, tartar, and plaque without damaging tooth enamel. This method is especially suitable for smokers, coffee or tea lovers, and those for whom aesthetic result is important.

Gum bleeding is the most common sign of gingivitis (gum inflammation). It is important to see a hygienist as soon as possible. Usually, professional cleaning and proper home care solve this problem within 1-2 weeks.

Yes, part of oral hygiene services can be compensated. For children under 18 and pregnant women, some procedures are fully compensated. For adults, part of hygiene-related treatment procedures may be compensated. We will provide detailed information during the consultation.
