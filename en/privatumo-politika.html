Privacy Policy

Here you will find information on how we collect, use, and store your personal data. We value your trust and ensure data security.

I

General Information

This Privacy Policy establishes the basic principles and procedures for personal data collection, processing and storage performed by the "Švytintys Dantys" clinic (hereinafter - the Data Controller).

We process your personal data in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the Law on Legal Protection of Personal Data of the Republic of Lithuania, and other legal acts regulating personal data protection.

II

What data do we collect?

To provide you with high-quality dental services, we may collect the following data:

  • Identification data: Name, surname, personal identification number, date of birth.
  • Contact details: Phone number, email address, residential address.
  • Special category (health) data: Information about your health condition, diagnoses, treatment plans, X-rays, photos, allergies, and used medications.
  • Payment data: Information about payments for services provided.
III

Purposes of data collection

We use your data for the following purposes:

Service Provision

For registration, diagnostics, treatment planning and treatment execution.

Communication

To confirm visits, remind about scheduled procedures or preventive check-ups.

Legal Obligations

To fulfill requirements for managing medical documentation required by laws.

Quality Improvement

To improve service quality and manage patient feedback.

IV

Data retention periods

Your personal and health data are stored for periods defined by the Ministry of Health of the Republic of Lithuania and other legal acts (usually 15 years for medical records).

Other data (e.g., inquiries) are stored for as long as necessary to achieve the goals or as established by internal clinic procedures, but not longer than 2 years.

V

Your rights

You have the right to:

  • Get acquainted with your personal data and how it is processed.
  • Demand correction of incorrect, incomplete or inaccurate personal data.
  • Demand deletion of data ("right to be forgotten"), if this does not contradict legal obligations to store medical data.
  • Restrict data processing actions.
  • Data portability.
  • Submit a complaint to the State Data Protection Inspectorate.
VI

Cookies and Changes

We use cookies on our website to ensure smooth operation and improve user experience. You can manage cookie settings in your browser.

We reserve the right to change this Privacy Policy. The latest version is always published on our website.
